Introduction
Bhagat Singh was a young revolutionary who fought for India's independence from British rule. He was born in 1907 in Banga, Punjab, British India (present-day Punjab, Pakistan). He was a brilliant student and was deeply influenced by the ideals of freedom and equality.
Early Life AND Activism
In 1923, Bhagat Singh joined the National College in Lahore. He was also a member of the Hindustan Republican Association (HRA), a revolutionary organization that was fighting for India's independence. In 1928, the British set up the Simon Commission to report on the political situation in India. The HRA decided to protest against the Commission's arrival in India. During a protest, Lala Lajpat Rai, a prominent leader of the HRA, was killed by a police officer. Bhagat Singh was enraged by Rai's death and vowed to avenge it.
The Lahore Conspiracy Case
In 1929, Bhagat Singh and his comrades threw a bomb in the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi. They did not intend to harm anyone, but they wanted to protest against the British rule. The bomb did not explode, but Bhagat Singh and his comrades were arrested. They were put on trial in the Lahore Conspiracy Case.
The Hanging
Bhagat Singh and his comrades were sentenced to death. They were hanged in the Lahore jail on March 23, 1931. Bhagat Singh was just 23 years old. His death was a major turning point in the Indian independence movement.
Legacy
Bhagat Singh is considered a martyr for India's independence. He is remembered for his courage, his commitment to justice, and his willingness to sacrifice his own life for the cause of freedom. He is an inspiration to people all over the world who are fighting for freedom and equality.
Slogans by Bhagat Singh
Bhagat Singh was a revolutionary who fought for India's independence from British rule. He was also a poet and a writer, and he often used slogans to spread his message of freedom and equality. Here are some of his most famous slogans:

  1. Inquilab Zindabad! (Long live the revolution!)
  2. Yeh jung nahi khatam hoga, jab tak azaadi nahi milegi! (This war will not end until we achieve freedom!)
  3. Bande mat kar! (Don't be afraid!)
  4. Mera rang dekh kar, gora hosh mein aa! (Look at my color and come to your senses, white man!)
  5. Aatma hi parmeshwara hai! (The soul is God!)
